A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The man who saved a billion lives

About the legend

  • 1931 - A.P.J Abdul Kalam in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam born on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 2002 - Served as the 11th President of India until 2007.
  • 1958 - Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from teh Madras Institute of Technology and join the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1969 - He moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-III the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.
  • 1981 - Kalam received honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushan for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
  • 1982 - Rejoining DRDO, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missle Man."
  • 1989 - Among those successes was Agni, India launch the first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III.
  • 1990 - Kalam received honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Vibhushan for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.
  • 1992 - Kalam was scientific adviser o the defense minister until 1997
  • 1997 - Kalam reveived India's highest civilian honour, the Bharat Ratna, for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 - He play a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear test.
  • 1998 - Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years. The plan called for, among other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.
  • 1999 - He served as principal scientific adviser to the government with the rank of cabinet minister until 2001.
  • 2013 - He was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".
  • 2015 - Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 July aged 83, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. Wheeler island, a missile test site in Odisha, was renamed Kalam Island in September. Also a prominent road in New Delhi was renamed from Aurangzeb Road to Dr APJ Abdul Kalam Road in August.
  • 2018 - Scientist from the Botanical Survey of India named a newly found plant species as Drypetes Kalamii, in his honour.
